Percy Jackson and the Olympians: The Lightning Thief Essay

By Jonah Gann

How Does the Book Differ from the Movie?

Once Percy makes it to camp in the movie, they reveal that Percy Jackson is Posidon’s son, because he built Percy a little house at the camp with tridents as decoration. While in the book, no one knows that Percy is Posidon’s son until a trident, made from water, floats above Percy’s head to protect him against a bully, named Clarissa. Clarissa was breaking the rules in Capture the Flag. In the movie this does not happen and Clarissa does not appear.

Later, Luke, one of the camp counselors, gives Percy, Grover, and Annabeth a map to each pearl they needed to escape the underworld later when they got there. This does not happen in the book. After that, Luke gives Percy a pair of flying shoes and a shield. In the book Percy gets the flying shoes but he doesn’t end up using them, instead Grover does. Also in the book, Luke never gives them a shield.

In the movie, they go on a quest and follow the map to get the pearls, while in the book, Percy is given the pearls by a water person sent by Posidon, and they just go to the locations on the way to the underworld.

The next difference, in the film, they go to a museum to get a pearl that is in a statue, but a hydra is guarding it and they fight the hydra. In the book, none of this happens, and instead they fight a Chimera on a tower and Percy jumps off it from very high into some water. Also, in the book, there is an entire bus fight scene with the three furies; in the movie none of this happens. Another difference is that in the book, Ares hides the lightning bolt in Percy’s backpack instead of Luke hiding it in Percy’s shield at the beginning of the movie. In the movie, Ares does not appear.

In the book, where Medusa appears, Medusa disguises herself as a nice lady and gives Percy, Grover, and Annabeth delicious food. In the movie, she is immediately revealed as Medusa and does not serve food. One major difference is that when Percy saves his mom she stays behind, but in the movie, instead Grover stays behind.

After they defeat Medusa in the book, they send Medusa’s head to Olympus, and at the end of the book, they ship it back to Percy’s house and his mom uses it to turn Percy’s abusive stepdad, Gabe, into a statue and she sells it. In the movie this does not happen and Percy’s mom just kicks Gabe out. In conclusion, these are the main differences that I have taken note of between the movie and the book of Percy Jackson and the Olympians: The Lightning Thief.

What couldn’t have been removed from the book in the movie because it would ruin the main idea?

One part of the story that couldn’t have been removed from the book in the movie because it would ruin the main idea is if Percy’s mom had not been captured. The two reasons this would not work is that, first, it would get rid of the motivation that Percy has on this quest, and they would not have to go to Hades.
